---
title: "Average Velocity"
date: 2026-07-05
description: "Displacement over elapsed time, and what that ratio hides."
---

Average velocity is displacement divided by the time it took:

$$
\bar{v} = \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t} = \frac{x_2 - x_1}{t_2 - t_1}
$$

On a position–time graph, $\bar{v}$ is the slope of the straight line
connecting the two points — a secant. The motion between them can be
arbitrarily messy; the average only sees the endpoints:
